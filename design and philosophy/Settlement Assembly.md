# The Secret Sauce

I think every city should ultimately be assembled from four layers.

```text
Terrain
↓
Infrastructure
↓
Buildings
↓
Decorations
```

So Ironhold might look like:

```text
Mountain
↓
River
↓
Road
↓
Walls
↓
Keep
↓
Library
↓
Observatory
↓
Market
↓
Banner
↓
Labels
```

Every city is unique.

---

## Tier 1 — Primitive Assets

These are your "building blocks."

Think of them like individual LEGO bricks.

* Individual tower
* Wall segment
* Roof
* Window
* Door
* Tree
* Rock
* Bush
* Banner
* Flag

These are the smallest pieces.

---

## Tier 2 — Composite Assets

Built from primitives.

* Cottage
* Castle
* Windmill
* Library
* Harbor
* Bridge
* Gatehouse

These are what you'll drag onto maps most of the time.

---

## Tier 3 — Landmark Assets

These represent concepts.

They're almost unique.

For example:

* The Great Archive
* The Observatory
* The Hall of Records
* The Guild of Engineers
* The Lighthouse of Dawn

These become recognizable landmarks within *your* world, tied to major dashboards or domains.

---

## Tier 4 — Regional Assets

These are whole compositions.

Examples:

* Entire city layouts
* Forest clusters
* Mountain ranges
* Harbor districts
* Farmland regions

These aren't single icons—they're reusable chunks of the world.
